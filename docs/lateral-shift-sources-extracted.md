## NOTEBOOK 1: Correcting the Lateral Pelvic Tilt and Lumbar Shift
*Sources: 10 YouTube videos on lateral pelvic tilt, lumbar shift correction, disc herniation exercises*

### Diagnostic & Assessment Protocols
- **Eyes-Closed Awareness Check:** Stand with feet hip-distance apart, chest lifted, arms relaxed. Close eyes for one minute to sense weight distribution (heavier foot), arm-to-hip spacing, shoulder/neck asymmetry.
- **Mirror Assessment:** Check if sternum is centered over navel. Look for skin folds in abdomen and "arm-to-body" space (in shift, one arm rests against hip while other has gap).
- **Movement Testing (Correction Test):** Attempt to manually pull trunk back over hips. If you cannot correct or maintain for a few minutes → true lateral shift confirmed.
- **Provocation Extension Test:** Perform 10 repeated backward extensions in standing. ⚠️ If symptoms worsen, move further down the leg, or shift becomes more visible → STOP extensions and perform lateral shift corrections first.

### Lateral Shift Correction Techniques
- **Manual Self-Correction (Standing):** ✅
  - If shifted left: left hand on lower ribs, right hand on right hip. Inhale tall; exhale — push ribs RIGHT and hips LEFT. Hold 5 seconds.
  - Frequency: Every 1–2 hours initially; 1–2×/day for maintenance.
- **Standing Side Glide (Wall-Supported):** ✅
  - Stand 8–12 inches from wall with feet together. Lean shoulder of NON-painful side against wall, elbow at 90°. Outside hand on hip, drive hips toward wall. Rhythmical — do NOT let hips return to shifted position between reps.
  - If hip hits wall before spine is straight → place pillow/spacer between shoulder and wall.
  - 10–15 reps/set. Every 1–2 hours (or 7–8×/day).
  - **Correct Wall Dismount:** Step foot CLOSEST to wall further toward wall before stepping away (prevents re-shift).
- **Lying Shift Correction (Unloaded):** ✅ (for extreme pain/sensitivity)
  - Lie on side toward which hips are shifting. Place foam roller/block under that hip to gravity-assist correction.
- **Locking in the Correction (Walking):** ✅
  - After manual correction, maintain overcorrected position and take steps for ~1 minute.

### Mobility & Decompression
- **Cat-Cow:** ✅
  - All fours — round back (Cat) then arch (Cow). Only round until you "kiss the pain" — not through it. Gradually increase range.
- **Lumbar Extensions (Prone Press-ups/Cobra):** ✅
  - Lie prone, push chest up with arms. Keep lower back and glutes RELAXED (do not squeeze).
  - Progression: flat → elbows → half press-up → full press-up.
  - Hold 10 sec/rest 10 sec, or 10 rhythmic reps. 7–8×/day every 2–3 hours.
  - ⚠️ Address lateral shift BEFORE performing. Do not squeeze glutes.
- **Extension in Standing:** ✅
  - Hands on small of back, arch backward. 10 reps, hold 1–2 sec each.
  - ⚠️ If increases leg pain or restricts movement → stop, return to side glides.
- **Extension while at the Wall:** ✅
  - After side glide at wall — while hips pressed against wall, arch upper body backward. 6 reps. 7–8×/day for 1–2 days.

### Stability & Strengthening
- **Single Leg Bear (All-Fours Hover):** ✅ Subacute phase
  - All fours, lift knees 2–3 inches off floor. Extend one leg back, squeeze glute, keep core still. Hold 2–3 sec. If too difficult, just hold the knees-up position.
- **Side Plank Clamshell (Injured Side Down):** ✅ Subacute phase
  - Partial side plank on knees, INJURED SIDE DOWN. Lift hips up and forward while opening top knee. Strengthens QL and glute medius to "lock in" corrected shift.
- **Dead Bug:** ✅ Subacute phase
  - Back on floor, arms and legs in air. Flatten lower back against floor. Slowly lower OPPOSITE arm and leg. Slow and controlled.
- **Partial Side Plank with Leg Kick:** ⚠️ Subacute (only if pain-free in clamshell)
  - Partial side plank, kick top leg up to even out pelvis.

### Nerve & Therapeutic Protocols
- **Sciatic Nerve Mobilization (Nerve Gliding/Flossing):** ⚠️ CRITICAL CAUTION
  - Lie on back, hold painful leg. Extend and flex toes toward face while looking up. Then bend knee, point toes down, chin down.
  - ⚠️ Do NOT perform until disc is "off the nerve" (pain centralized). Performing while disc still on nerve causes further irritation.
- **Centralization Process:** ✅ Primary treatment goal
  - Choose exercises that move pain from leg/extremity toward spine center. Central back pain may temporarily increase — this is acceptable if leg pain moves closer to spine.
- **Spinal Decompression:** ⚠️ Use caution
  - Inversion tables, decompression machines — STOP if pain worsens. Can pull on sensitive nerves or exacerbate instability.

## NOTEBOOK 2: Back Recovery & Fat Loss — March 2026
*Sources: McKenzie Manual PDF, OrthoNC Lumbar Extension PDF, McKenzie JCDR Review PDF, YouTube videos (lateral shift, standing abs, herniated disc), Spine-health articles, MS Trust Core Stability PDF, NASA DeskFit PDF, CoachSofia standing core, Welltech cardio article*

### McKenzie Method (MDT) Procedures

**Prone Lying (Procedure 1):** ✅
- Lie face down, arms at sides, head turned to one side. Relax. Allow lower back to sag into extension. Sustained up to 3 minutes. Every 2–3 hours.
- ⚠️ If improvement not maintained or acute lumbar kyphosis exists → pillow under abdomen, remove gradually.

**Prone Lying in Extension (Procedure 2):** ✅
- Prone, elbows under shoulders, support on forearms. Keep hips on floor. Relax lower back. Up to 3 minutes. Every 2–3 hours.

**Posture Correction (Procedure 4):** ✅
- Move from slouched (kyphotic) sitting to upright by anteriorly rotating pelvis, accentuating lordosis, lifting chest. Maintain with lumbar roll. Constant monitoring.

**Extension in Lying (EIL) — Cobra Press-up (Procedure 5):** ✅
- Prone, hands palm-down under shoulders. Straighten elbows to push chest up. Keep pelvis and thighs RELAXED. Lock elbows, breathe out at top. Return. 10 reps/set. Every 2–3 hours.
- ⚠️ Care when resuming upright posture — maintain lordosis.

**Extension in Standing (EIS) (Procedure 9):** ✅
- Feet shoulder-width. Hands in small of back. Lean backward as far as possible using hands as fulcrum. Return. 10 reps. Every 2–3 hours; use proactively before pain starts during prolonged sitting/bending.

**Slouch-Overcorrect (Procedure 10):** ✅
- Sit on chair edge in fully slouched posture. Move to maximal lordosis (rotate pelvis, lift chest). Hold 1–2 sec. Release last 10% of strain = correct sitting posture. 10–15 reps/session. 3×/day to re-educate postural habits.

**Extension in Lying with Hips Off Centre (Procedure 11):** ✅
- Prone. Shift hips AWAY from painful side (if right-sided pain → shift hips left). Perform standard EIL press-ups. 10 reps.
- ⚠️ Hips tend to straighten during set — may need re-adjustment.

**Self-Correction of Lateral Shift / Side Gliding (Procedure 16):** ✅
- Against wall: Stand with pain-FREE side ~2 feet from wall. Lean shoulder against wall, elbow bent. Other hand on pelvis. Push pelvis horizontally toward wall until centered. Hold 1–2 sec. 10–15 reps.
- Standing: feet shoulder-width, glide hips laterally while keeping shoulders level.
- Doorway: stabilize upper trunk against frame, move hips laterally.
- Every 1–2 hours or anytime shift returns. Follow with Extension in Standing.
- ⚠️ Stop if peripheralization occurs.

**Flexion in Lying (Procedure 18):** ⚠️ Anterior derangements only
- Supine, knees/hips flexed. Bring knees toward chest with hands. 10 reps. Every 2–3 hours for anterior derangements.
- ⚠️ Follow with extension procedures to ensure no posterior derangement provoked.

**Rotation in Flexion (Procedure 23):** ✅ (clinician guidance recommended)
- Supine, knees/hips at 90°. Lower knees laterally to table. Sustained up to 3 minutes. Most commonly rotate toward painful side.

### Standing Core Stability & Abdominal Exercises

**Standing Knee Lift:** ✅
- Stand tall, feet hip-width. Tighten core "as if preparing for light punch." Lift one knee to hip height slowly. Torso upright. 8–10 reps/side.
- ⚠️ Keep pelvis level; avoid arching lower back.

**Standing Knee Tap:** ✅
- Stand upright. Lift one leg slightly, tap knee with opposite hand.

**Standing Woodchopper:** ✅ (no weight or very light)
- Feet shoulder-width. Hands together overhead. Rotate torso diagonally downward behind opposite knee. Pivot on foot, not hips.

**Standing Knee to Elbow Reach (Same Side):** ✅
- Hands behind head. Reach one knee toward elbow on same side.
- ⚠️ Don't bend sideways excessively; move slowly.

**Knee to Opposite Elbow (Standing Core Crunch):** ✅
- Hands behind head. Lift one knee, rotate torso to bring opposite elbow toward knee.
- ⚠️ Avoid pulling on neck; focus on core.

**Single Leg Balance and Reach:** ✅
- Stand on one foot. Move lifted foot forward, to side, all the way back — no side-bending torso.

**Standing Bird Dog:** ✅
- Raise one arm forward while flexing opposite hip/knee. Alternate sides slowly.

**Side to Side Rotations:** ✅ (with light weight 4 lbs max)
- Brace core/glutes. Hold light weight in front. Rotate side to side.

**Single Leg Forward and Reach:** ✅
- Weight on one leg. Hinge at hips, push other leg back (not above glutes), stretch arms forward.

**High Knee Lifts / Marches:** ✅
- March in place, knees to hip height. Engage core. No bouncing.

**Hip Hinge to Side Leg Lift:** ✅
- Hinge at hips. Return to standing, lift one leg to side.

**Backward Lunge to Side Rotation:** ⚠️ Subacute only
- Cross arms. Lunge back, rotate torso over front leg.

### Progressive Core Stability (MS Trust Protocol)

**Table Top Base:** ✅ All phases
- Supine, neutral spine. Pull tummy in, engage pelvic floor. Breathe.

**Table Top Level 1:** ✅
- Lift one foot to 90/90 without losing neutral spine.

**Table Top Level 2:** ✅
- Both legs tabletop; lower one foot to touch floor then return.

**4-Point Kneeling (Levels 1–4):** ✅ Progressive
- Level 1: Hands/knees, neutral spine, core engaged.
- Level 2: Slide one foot back along floor.
- Level 3: Lift leg.
- Level 4: Lift opposite arm and leg simultaneously.

**Bridge Level 1:** ✅ Subacute phase
- Supine. Peel spine off floor bone by bone. Hold 2 sec at top. Lower slowly. Do NOT hyperextend.

**Bridge Level 2:** ✅ Subacute (pain-free for 2 sessions first)
- Hold bridge, straighten one leg forward while holding. Keep hips level.

**Clamshells (Sidelying):** ✅
- Sidelying, knees bent, heels together. Lift top knee — keep heels touching. Glute medius.

**Dead Bug:** ✅ All subacute phases
- Same as above — back flat, opposite arm/leg lower slowly.

**One Leg Stretch (Levels 1–2):** ✅
- Supine, slide one heel forward to straighten leg (Level 1). Level 2: from tabletop, extend leg diagonally.

**Horizontal Hold (Plank Progressions):** ⚠️ Subacute/strength phase only
- Level 0: Prone on elbows, lift tummy. Level 1: lift hips to knees. Level 2: full plank on toes.

**Single Leg Drop Out:** ✅
- Supine. Roll one leg outward slowly while keeping pelvis level.

**Straight Leg Side Lifts:** ✅ Subacute
- Sidelying. Lift top leg to hip height. Level 2: lift lower leg to meet top.

### Other Specific Rehab Exercises

**Waiter's Bow:** ✅
- Stand and hinge at hips maintaining natural lumbar curve. Squeeze glutes to return. Trains spine-neutral hip hinge.

**Seated Hamstring Stretch:** ✅
- Chair, extend one leg (heel on floor), lean forward from shoulders — NO back rounding. Natural lumbar curve maintained.

**Standing Backbend:** ✅
- Hands on low back above buttocks. Drive hips forward, arch backward. Hold 1 sec.

**Prone Leg Raises:** ✅ (gentle)
- Lie prone with pillow under hips. Raise one leg 1–6 inches off floor.

### Desk/Office Exercises (NASA DeskFit)
*(Appropriate for all phases — zero spinal load)*

- **Seated Marches:** Run in spot sitting, 15–20 sec.
- **Seated Leg Extensions:** Lift straight leg, no back rounding. 10 reps.
- **Seated Reverse Shoulder Fly:** Lean forward, arch back, raise arms. 15 reps.
- **Standing Calf Raises:** Behind chair. 12–15 reps.
- **Seated Torso Twists:** Lean back slightly, elbows at ribs level, twist. 20 reps.
- **Chair Sit and Stand:** Squat to chair without touching. 10–15 reps.
- **Desk Push-ups:** Hands on desk edge. 8–15 reps.
- **Standing Leg Curl:** Foot to backside. 10–15 reps.
- **Desk Plank:** Forearms on desk, toes on floor, straight line. 10–15 sec.
- **Seated Neck Stretch:** Chin to chest/ceiling/sides. 10–15 sec each.
- **Seated Lean Stretch:** One arm overhead, bend to side. 10–15 sec, 2 sets.
- **Seated Chest and Back Stretch:** Clasp hands behind back; then clasp in front. 10–15 sec.

### Cardio Guidelines (Spine-Safe)

- **Walking:** ✅ Natural upright gait. 20–30 min most days.
- **Swimming:** ✅ Backstroke/breaststroke gentler than freestyle.
- **Stationary/Recumbent Bike:** ✅ Recumbent provides better lumbar support.
- **Elliptical:** ✅ Feet never leave pedals. Mimics walking without impact.
- **Tai Chi:** ✅ Slow controlled motions for postural strength.
- **Low-Impact HIIT Swaps:**
  - Mini Jumping Lunges (no jump, limited range) ✅
  - Incline Mountain Climbers (hands elevated, slow) ✅
  - Quiet Burpees (step one leg at a time, no jump) ✅
  - High Knee Marches ✅

## Critical Cautions & Red Flags

**Stop immediately if:**
- Pain spreads further DOWN the leg (peripheralization) — stop all extension exercises
- Loss of bladder/bowel control → emergency
- Loss of sensation in legs/saddle area → emergency
- Global weakness or clumsy legs → emergency
- Fever, night pain, malaise → see doctor

**Do NOT perform McKenzie for:**
- Serious spinal pathology (cancer, fractures, infections)
- Cauda equina syndrome / severe spinal cord compression
- Recent traumatic injury (until fracture ruled out)
- Post-spinal fusion surgery (during healing)

**Avoid during flare-ups:**
- High-impact running, plyometrics, fast-paced HIIT, contact sports
- Flexion exercises within first few hours of waking

**Sequence rule:** Always correct the lateral shift BEFORE extension exercises. Never skip straight to cobra/press-ups.
